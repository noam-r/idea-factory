# Phase I Summary

Idea Factory Phase I is a guided concept-development system for independent thinkers working on product, framework, or policy concepts.

Its job is not to generate hype, produce endless brainstorming, or behave like a free-form CMS. Its job is to help a user take a rough idea, make it clearer, evaluate it honestly, and turn it into a small concept artifact that can be used privately or shared with others.

## The core promise

Phase I helps a user:

1. understand the idea
2. evaluate the idea
3. structure the idea
4. compile a concept artifact
5. share it if warranted

The value order matters. The system should first help the user understand what they mean, then judge whether it is worth more effort, and only then help turn it into a shareable artifact.

## Who Phase I is for

Phase I is designed for independent thinkers working on:

- product concepts
- framework concepts
- policy concepts

It is especially suited for users who have strong ideas but need help:

- clarifying them
- stress-testing them
- organizing them
- presenting them clearly

## The workflow

Phase I uses a six-stage workflow:

1. Capture
2. Clarify
3. Structure
4. Evaluate
5. Compile
6. Publish

Each stage has a distinct purpose, clear outputs, and clear stopping conditions.

## What makes Phase I different

Phase I is defined by several deliberate choices:

- the user owns the concept
- the LLM acts like a calm, deliberate mentor
- the LLM may refuse to move forward when clarity is too low
- evaluation is built into the artifact instead of added as an afterthought
- editing is prompt-based and governed, not free-form
- drift is handled through linked projects, not branching
- session length is controlled through the Progress Efficiency Model
- the system prefers honest stopping points over endless continuation

## What the user gets

At the end of a successful Phase I project, the user should have:

- a clear concept definition
- a problem and audience framing
- a small structured concept artifact
- an Evaluation page with recommendation
- a visible list of open questions
- assumptions and risks
- a clear next action
- optionally a publishable concept site for a chosen audience

## What Phase I is not trying to do

Phase I is not trying to solve everything.

It is not primarily about:

- technical architecture
- implementation design
- infrastructure or hosting
- full market validation
- billing implementation
- organization-scale collaboration
- unrestricted content management

Those can come later if the user chooses to move from concept formation into later stages of work.

## Why Phase I matters

Phase I solves a specific problem: many good ideas remain vague, over-polished, under-evaluated, or trapped inside long documents and chats.

Idea Factory Phase I gives those ideas a disciplined path:

- from ambiguity to clarity
- from enthusiasm to evaluation
- from conversation to concept artifact

That is the point of the phase.
