# Who It Is For

## Primary audience

Phase I of **Idea Factory** is designed for **independent thinkers** working on concepts that are still forming and need disciplined clarification.

The clearest first-fit users are people developing:

- product concepts,
- framework concepts,
- policy concepts.

These users usually do not need a blank document or a generic brainstorming chat. They need help turning a rough idea into something clearer, more evaluable, and more explainable.

## What kind of user this describes

The target user is not defined by job title. The target user is defined by the kind of work they are trying to do.

A good Phase I user is someone who:

- has an idea that is not yet fully clear,
- wants to understand it better before overcommitting,
- wants honest critique instead of empty encouragement,
- wants a structured artifact rather than a long unstructured document,
- and may eventually want to share the concept with collaborators, peers, or a trusted audience.

This can include:

- an independent builder shaping a product idea,
- a strategist or consultant shaping a framework,
- a researcher or writer shaping a conceptual model,
- a policy thinker shaping a governance or institutional idea,
- or a general independent operator trying to make sense of a concept before taking it further.

## What these users usually struggle with

These users often face the same problems:

### The idea is still too vague

They can feel the shape of the concept, but they cannot yet explain it clearly.

### The idea expands too quickly

As they think, the concept grows in many directions at once. The result is often confusion rather than clarity.

### Existing tools reward accumulation more than judgment

A note app or large document may preserve thought, but it does not necessarily help the user decide:

- what the idea actually is,
- whether it is worth further effort,
- what should be kept,
- what should be cut,
- and what should become a separate project.

### Sharing too early is risky

If the concept is still vague, sharing it in document form often creates misunderstanding or gives the illusion of completeness.

Idea Factory is meant to improve that situation by guiding the concept through clarification, structure, and evaluation before publication is treated as a default next step.

## What kind of user Phase I is not optimized for

Phase I is intentionally narrow.

It is **not** primarily optimized for:

### Users who only want free-form writing tools

If the goal is unrestricted direct editing, creative drafting, or simple document writing, a conventional editor or CMS may be a better fit.

### Users who only want brainstorming with no structure

Idea Factory is not designed to keep a user in an endless exploration loop.

It is designed to move toward clarity, judgment, and a visible next step.

### Users who mainly need implementation design

If the user already knows what the concept is and primarily needs architecture, API design, hosting decisions, or engineering planning, that belongs after Phase I.

### Users who want to force positive evaluation

The system is deliberately not built to flatter, overrate, or cosmetically improve weak ideas without preserving honest judgment.

## Why independent thinkers are the right first audience

Independent thinkers are a strong first audience because they often need both:

- a thinking aid,
- and a communication artifact.

They may not have a team, a process, or a structured internal review system. That makes them especially vulnerable to:

- over-investing in weak ideas,
- staying too long in vague thinking,
- or creating large artifacts that look serious without actually clarifying the concept.

Idea Factory is well matched to this because it provides:

- a guided process,
- an honest evaluation layer,
- and a small concept artifact that is easier to think with and easier to share.

## Product, framework, and policy concepts

Phase I focuses on three concept families because they are different enough to need structure, but similar enough to share the same core workflow.

### Product concepts

These usually need help answering questions such as:

- what problem is being solved,
- who the user is,
- what the mechanism is,
- whether the idea is differentiated,
- and whether it is defensible enough to justify effort.

### Framework concepts

These usually need help answering questions such as:

- what mental model is being proposed,
- what the core principles are,
- where the framework applies,
- and whether it provides real explanatory value.

### Policy concepts

These usually need help answering questions such as:

- what stakeholder problem is being addressed,
- how the mechanism works,
- what tradeoffs exist,
- and whether the concept remains coherent under institutional constraints.

## What the user should expect from the system

A Phase I user should expect the system to:

- ask for clarification,
- ask for examples when explanations are weak,
- push back on vague or unsupported thinking,
- refuse premature polish,
- provide explicit evaluation,
- and make stopping or pausing legitimate outcomes.

The system should not behave like an enthusiastic assistant trying to preserve user motivation at all costs. It should behave like a calm senior guide trying to improve the quality of thought.

## What success looks like for this user

For the target user, success in Phase I does not necessarily mean public publication.

Success may mean:

- the idea is now understandable,
- the idea has been honestly evaluated,
- the strongest version of the concept is now visible,
- a weak idea was stopped before more effort was wasted,
- or a clearer linked project emerged from a scattered original idea.

That is why the audience fit is strong: these users need help not just creating artifacts, but deciding what deserves further energy.

## Summary

Idea Factory Phase I is for independent thinkers who need help turning a rough product, framework, or policy concept into something:

- clearer,
- more structured,
- more honestly evaluated,
- and more shareable when appropriate.

It is not for users who mainly want unrestricted editing, endless brainstorming, or immediate technical design. It is for users who want disciplined conceptual progress.
