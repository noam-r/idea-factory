# Concept Artifact Model

Idea Factory Phase I produces a **small concept artifact** rather than a giant document or a free-form notes dump.

The artifact is designed to help a user:

1. understand the idea
2. evaluate the idea
3. share the idea in a structured way

It should remain small, readable, and honest.

## What the artifact is

The standard output is a **concept site** or **idea brief with pages**.

It is not primarily:

- a technical design document
- a marketing site
- a free-form wiki
- a CMS-managed publication space

The artifact exists to express the current state of a concept clearly enough for reflection, evaluation, and sharing.

## Design principles

### Small by default

A first artifact should be compact. It should usually contain a small set of core pages rather than a sprawling site.

### Layered

The artifact should support:

- quick understanding
- deeper explanation
- honest evaluation
- clear next steps

### Honest

It should preserve:

- open questions
- assumptions
- risks
- limitations
- recommendation state

The artifact should not pretend certainty where none exists.

### Type-aware

A product concept, framework concept, and policy concept should not all look identical. They should share a common backbone while allowing type-specific emphasis.

### Publishable but disciplined

The artifact may be shared, but it should not become performative or hype-driven.

## What the artifact should help answer

A good artifact should let a reader answer:

- what is this?
- what problem does it address?
- how does it work?
- who is it for?
- what seems strong or weak about it?
- what remains unresolved?
- what should happen next?

## Relationship to the process

The artifact is not the whole system. It is one output of the system.

It sits on top of:

- the workflow
- the evaluation model
- the governance model
- the progress model
- the concept history

That means the artifact is visible and readable, while the deeper system preserves decisions, milestones, recommendations, and traceability.

## Artifact maturity

The artifact should also have a maturity state. In Phase I, useful maturity labels include:

- rough concept
- structured concept
- evaluated concept
- draft concept site
- publishable concept site
- published concept site

This helps the user understand what kind of object they currently have.

## Phase I boundary

Phase I artifacts should focus on concept quality and concept communication.

They do **not** need to include:

- detailed technical architecture
- implementation plans
- infrastructure or hosting concerns
- security details
- large-scale market analysis
- detailed monetization logic

Those may appear later if the user chooses to continue beyond concept formation.

## Why this model matters

Without a defined artifact model, the product risks collapsing into:

- a long chat transcript
- a generic AI document generator
- a site builder without conceptual discipline

The artifact model keeps the output small, legible, and trustworthy.
