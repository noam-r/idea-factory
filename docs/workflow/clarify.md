# Clarify

## Purpose of Clarify

Clarify is the stage where the project stops being a vague idea and starts becoming a defined concept.

Its purpose is to give the idea a stable center.

By the end of Clarify, the system should be able to say, with reasonable confidence:

- what the idea is,
- who it is for,
- what problem or opportunity it addresses,
- how it works at a concept level,
- and what is outside its scope.

## Why Clarify matters so much

Clarify is one of the most important stages in the entire workflow.

If Clarify is weak, later stages become misleading.

A weak Clarify stage often leads to:

- structure built on confusion,
- evaluation built on guesswork,
- and compiled artifacts that look clearer than the concept really is.

This is why the system should be willing to stay in Clarify until the idea is stable enough to move on.

## What the LLM should do in Clarify

In Clarify mode, the LLM should act like a careful senior guide.

It should:

- ask the highest-leverage questions only,
- explain why each question matters,
- narrow vague language,
- help separate core concept from noise,
- identify what the idea is not,
- test the boundaries of the idea,
- and detect whether drift is occurring.

The system should aim for precision, not quantity.

## What the LLM should not do in Clarify

Clarify should not become:

- endless brainstorming,
- a broad idea-generation session,
- an excuse to keep asking questions without convergence,
- or a premature transition into writing pages.

If the session keeps moving without creating clarity, the system should say so.

## What Clarify is trying to define

A strong Clarify stage should produce clear statements about:

### The concept itself

What is the idea, in plain language?

### The audience

Who is this for? Who is affected by it? Who is not the intended user or stakeholder?

### The problem or opportunity

What is the thing this concept is trying to improve, solve, or make possible?

### The mechanism

How does it work at the level needed for this phase?

### The boundary

What belongs inside this concept, and what does not?

## Why examples matter in Clarify

Examples are often essential in Clarify.

If the user struggles to explain the concept directly, the system should ask for:

- a concrete example,
- a use case,
- a counterexample,
- or something similar that helps anchor the idea.

Examples can reveal:

- who the concept is actually for,
- what the real mechanism is,
- where the boundary should sit,
- and whether the user's words match their intention.

The system should still interpret examples carefully, but it should use them actively as a clarification tool.

## What Clarify should produce

A successful Clarify stage should usually produce:

- a clear concept definition,
- a problem statement,
- an audience statement,
- a mechanism summary,
- a scope or boundary statement,
- and a list of unresolved but visible questions.

This is enough to support the next stage.

## Clarify and convergence

Clarify is also where drift often becomes visible.

The system should monitor whether the project is:

- becoming more precise,
- splitting into multiple ideas,
- replacing its original core,
- or simply getting more confused.

If a materially different idea emerges, the system should say so explicitly.

The right response is not silent mutation. The right response is to:

- narrow the current project,
- or prepare to create a linked project.

## Clarify and session efficiency

Clarify can be longer than Capture, but it should still show clear progress.

A healthy Clarify session should gradually reduce:

- ambiguity,
- scope confusion,
- and conceptual drift.

If many interactions pass without better clarity, better boundaries, or better understanding, the session should be treated as slowing or stalled.

This is where the Progress Efficiency Model should help the system decide whether to:

- ask for one more example,
- force a narrowing decision,
- pause,
- or stop.

## What blocks Clarify

Clarify should block forward movement when:

- the audience is still too vague,
- the problem is still too vague,
- the mechanism is still too vague,
- the concept keeps mutating without being named,
- or the project no longer has a stable center.

The system should not move to Structure until it can honestly say the idea is stable enough to organize.

## What successful Clarify feels like

To the user, successful Clarify should feel like:

- the idea is finally becoming sayable,
- the core distinction is visible,
- the scope is narrower and cleaner,
- and the next stage now makes sense.

That is the practical test.

## Summary

Clarify is the stage where Idea Factory turns a rough concept seed into a defined idea with a stable center.

It does this by asking focused questions, using examples when needed, defining boundaries, and refusing to let the project move forward before the idea is clear enough to structure honestly.
